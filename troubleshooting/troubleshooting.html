First Layer not Binding (loose / unconnected filament tracks)

Sample Corrective Actions Discussion
  • Re-level bed and get the nozzle closer to the bed.
  • Increase the flow rate of the initial layer.
 If the nozzle is too high above the bed, the filament is not squished out enough. If the nozzle is at the proper height then either the line with or flow rate is too low. Increasing the line width is less ideal because it can effect other parameters such as the number of lines in the brim and position of the lines. A better choice is to increase the flow rate of the initial layer which will simply make the lines fatter.

Elephant's Foot

Visual Corrective Actions Discussion
  • Lower the bed temperature.
 The bed temperature is too high and the first layer is not solidifying before the weight of the layers above squish it. Here we can see a minor case. Note this artifact is inherent of printing on a heated bed. It may or may not be an issue depending on the print. For example it may lead to non-functional/frozen bearings.

Adhesion Bubbling

Visual Corrective Actions Discussion
  • Increase the bed temperature.
  • Decrease brim/initial layer speeds.
 Initially was successful in resolving this issue by increasing the bed temperature. Later tweaked the brim by reducing the line count to 10 and found I also needed to reduce the speed 10mm/s to maintain good adhesion. Furthermore at some point I was tweaking settings to try and eliminate a slight case of Elephant's Foot. Could the cube have been rocking which compromised the adhesion?

Nozzle Dragging (Poor Layering)

Visual Corrective Actions Discussion
  • Re-level bed.
 In this case, the back of the bed was slightly lower than the front. This cased multiple issues including:
  • Poorly laminated layers; visually not-aligned and the post snapped right off
  • The piece was printed with a brim which was bound to the piece more than normal and did not remove easily or cleanly.

Slow Data Transfer (USB)

Visual Corrective Actions Discussion
  • Print from SD card.
 This issue is more pronounced when printing cylindrical models due to the rapid incremental changes associated with round paths. Here is the same print done directly from an SD card.

I actually encountered this issue when printing to my Creality3D CR-10 V2 from OctoPrint. The first indication that something was the real time print completion time was fluctuating and that the actual print time was over an hour longer than originally estimated. The final print(Roller bearing from 100% printable filament spool holder) was fused and non functional. The surface also showed noticeable blobs/zits. Upon observing the second printing from the SD card, the motion of the print head was noticeably smoother and the outcome speaks for itself. Further more, the print completed in the expected time frame.

 This technique works well when printing with a brim or a raft.
